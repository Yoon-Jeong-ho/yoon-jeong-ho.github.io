NLP / LLM Research

Efficient and trustworthy LLM systems through post-hoc representation control.

Jeongho Yoon studies how pretrained language models can retrieve more reliably, preserve privacy, expose uncertainty, and run with lower computational cost.

My work focuses on making pretrained language models usable under practical constraints: lower compute, reliable retrieval, privacy-preserving inference, calibrated uncertainty, and safer behavior. Rather than treating LLMs as fixed black boxes or simply scaling them up, I study post-hoc methods for transforming, editing, compressing, and evaluating their representations.

Research Directions

A representation-centered research agenda

Representation Control

  • Language identity removal

Efficient Retrieval Systems

Practical methods for privacy-preserving inference, post-hoc uncertainty estimation, and adversarial safety evaluation.
